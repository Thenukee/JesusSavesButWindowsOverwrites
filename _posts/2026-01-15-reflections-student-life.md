University is a strange and wonderful time. You're constantly learning, constantly questioning, and — if you're lucky — constantly building. Here are some reflections from my journey so far.

## Finding Your Intersection

The best advice I ever received was: **don't pick one thing — find the intersection of several things you care about.**

For me, that intersection is:
- **Cybersecurity** — the technical challenge of protecting systems
- **Peace & Conflict Studies** — understanding why people and nations clash
- **Writing** — making complex ideas accessible

None of these fields alone defines me, but together they create a unique perspective that guides my research, my projects, and my career path.

## The Power of Building

There's a massive difference between *knowing* something and *building* something. Reading about cryptographic hashing is one thing. Building a file integrity monitor that actually uses it is another.

Every project I've completed has taught me more than any lecture. Not because lectures aren't valuable — they are — but because building forces you to confront the messy, real-world details that textbooks gloss over.

## Writing as Thinking

I started this blog not because I thought anyone would read it, but because **writing forces clarity**. When I write about a concept, I discover the gaps in my understanding. The act of explaining something to others is the best way to truly learn it.

If you're a student reading this, I encourage you: **start writing**. It doesn't have to be perfect. It doesn't have to be public at first. But put your thoughts into words and watch how much clearer your thinking becomes.

## What's Next

I'm heading into my final semester with a lot of exciting work ahead:
- Finishing my research projects
- Publishing more tools on GitHub
- Writing more frequently
- Figuring out what comes after graduation

Whatever comes next, I know the foundation I've built — technical skills, research capability, and the habit of writing — will serve me well.
