Studying at the General Sir John Kotelawala Defence University has been a unique experience. Here are some reflections on the journey so far.

## The Environment

KDU is unlike typical universities. The discipline, structure, and focus on national relevance shape everything — from how courses are designed to the expectations placed on students. It's challenging, but it builds resilience.

## Finding My Path

When I started, I wasn't sure exactly what I wanted to focus on. Computer science is vast. But over time, two areas kept pulling me in:

1. **Cybersecurity** — the technical challenge of protecting systems
2. **Peace & conflict studies** — the human challenge of building a better world

Finding the intersection of these two has become my mission.

## Challenges

Studying in Sri Lanka means dealing with:

- Limited access to some tools and resources
- A smaller community of peers in niche research areas
- The need to be self-directed in learning beyond the curriculum

But these challenges also push you to be resourceful, independent, and creative.

## What Keeps Me Going

- The excitement of solving a hard problem
- The satisfaction of writing something that helps others
- The belief that technology can be a force for peace
- A supportive community of fellow learners online

## Looking Ahead

I'm excited about the next phase — more research, more building, more writing. This blog is part of that commitment to sharing and growing in public.

If you're on a similar journey, reach out. I'd love to connect.
