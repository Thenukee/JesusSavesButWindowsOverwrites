At first glance, cybersecurity and peace studies might seem like completely different worlds. One deals with firewalls and encryption; the other with diplomacy and conflict resolution. But look deeper, and the connections are striking.

## Trust as a Foundation

Both fields revolve around **trust**. In cybersecurity, we build systems of trust through authentication, certificates, and verified identities. In peace studies, trust-building between conflicting parties is essential for lasting peace.

When trust breaks down in either domain, the consequences are severe.

## Structural Violence and Digital Inequality

Johan Galtung's concept of **structural violence** — harm caused by systems rather than direct action — has a clear parallel in the digital world. Consider:

- Communities without access to secure communication tools
- Populations vulnerable to surveillance without digital literacy
- The digital divide as a form of structural inequality

## Cyber Conflict as Modern Conflict

Nation-state cyber operations are now a regular feature of international relations. Understanding these through a peace studies lens helps us think about:

- **Deterrence** — how does cyber deterrence compare to nuclear deterrence?
- **Escalation** — when does a cyberattack cross the threshold of armed conflict?
- **Norms** — what international norms should govern cyber behavior?

## Building Digital Peace

Just as peacebuilding involves creating institutions and norms for peaceful coexistence, **digital peacebuilding** involves:

1. Developing international cyber norms
2. Building capacity for secure communication in conflict zones
3. Creating inclusive digital governance structures
4. Education and awareness programs

## What I'm Working On

This intersection is where I want to focus my research. I believe that cybersecurity professionals need frameworks from peace studies, and peace scholars need to understand the digital dimension of modern conflict.

> "Peace is not merely the absence of war. It is the presence of justice, of law, of order — in short, of government." — Albert Einstein

The same applies to cyberspace. Security isn't just the absence of attacks — it's the presence of resilient, equitable, and trustworthy systems.
